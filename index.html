Current Research

My research focuses these days on self-supervised and multi-modal machine learning techniques for vision+radar perception, including representation learning. I continue to occasionally contribute to wireless problems, particularly those intersecting with machine learning.

I have led a programme (over a period of 3 years) that envisioned and delivered a self-supervised learning algorithm for automotive radar perception. Our algorithm uses contrastive learning and domain-specific radar augmentations to provide substantial improvements over supervised learning: 70% in label efficiency and 5.8% in mAP. Performance characterised in the real-world under nontrivial MIMO radar artefacts.
